## Design Context

### Users
This product is for individual users who want to quickly capture ideas, tasks, and meaningful moments during the day. They use it like a lightweight writing tool, not a heavy knowledge system or enterprise dashboard.

### Brand Personality
Calm, precise, credible. The interface should feel dependable and thoughtfully designed, with enough personality to feel memorable but never noisy.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is a refined writing workspace with an editorial tool-desk character. Default presentation should be light mode, with a matching dark mode available. The light theme should feel clean and professional rather than playful or glossy, using warm paper-tinted neutrals, restrained contrast, and a disciplined accent color. The dark theme should preserve the current moody, minimal atmosphere.

### Design Principles
1. Writing comes first: the interface should support capture and review without decorative clutter.
2. Theme parity matters: light and dark modes should feel intentionally designed, not like one theme is a quick inversion of the other.
3. Restraint over spectacle: use contrast, typography, and spacing to create polish instead of heavy chrome or exaggerated effects.
4. Small controls, clear hierarchy: top-level actions should stay compact and legible, especially in the dashboard header.
5. Warm utility: even productivity surfaces should feel human and welcoming rather than cold or corporate.
